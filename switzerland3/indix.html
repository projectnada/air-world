tourism in switzerland

Switzerland is a geographical location in the middle of the European continent, a charming nature and a distinctive climate. What is left more to be one of the most beautiful tourism destinations in Europe and the world?! Thousands of tourists visit Switzerland as one of the richest countries in the world and to enjoy the beauty of its nature and spend an unforgettable vacation in one of Switzerland's wonderful cities such as the wonderful capital Geneva Switzerland is bordered by Germany, Austria, Italy and France, and this has strengthened its position on the international tourism map

Cuisine

The cuisine of Switzerland is multifaceted. While some dishes such as fondue, raclette or rösti are omnipresent through the country, each region developed its own gastronomy according to the differences of climate and languages. Traditional Swiss cuisine uses ingredients similar to those in other European countries, as well as unique dairy products and cheeses such as Gruyère or Emmental, produced in the valleys of Gruyères and Emmental. The number of fine-dining establishments is high, particularly in western Switzerland

sport

Skiing, snowboarding and mountaineering are among the most popular sports in Switzerland, the nature of the country being particularly suited for such activities Winter sports are practised by the natives and tourists since the second half of the 19th century with the invention of bobsleigh in St. Moritz,The most prominently watched sports in Switzerland are football, ice hockey, Alpine skiing, "Schwingen", and tennis.Hornussen is another indigenous Swiss sport, which is like a cross between baseball and golf. Steinstossen is the Swiss variant of stone put, a competition in throwing a heavy stone. Practised only among the alpine population since prehistoric times

Climate

The Swiss climate is generally temperate, but can vary greatly between the localities,from glacial conditions on the mountaintops to the often pleasant near Mediterranean climate at Switzerland's southern tip. There are some valley areas in the southern part of Switzerland where some cold-hardy palm trees are found. Summers tend to be warm and humid at times with periodic rainfall, so they are ideal for pastures and grazing. The less humid winters in the mountains may see long intervals of stable conditions for weeks. At the same time, the lower lands tend to suffer from inversion, during these periods, thus seeing no sun for weeks.

tourism

Tourism is one of the traditional sources of income in Switzerland, although the Swiss who travel abroad spend almost as much as foreign tourists who visit Switzerland.Switzerland has summer and winter resorts, and many resorts that can be recreated in both seasons. As for the period between winter and summer, it is filled with luxury resort tourism and conference tourism. One of the latest tourist fashions is a relaxing and wellness holiday trips often rely on mountain railways and lake boats. Mountain restaurants are also in high demand, at the expense of the traditional hotel sector
