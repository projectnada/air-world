Pictures of places to visit in Holland

ourism in Rotterdam

Rotterdam has many tourist attractions that make it an important destination for tourists coming to the Netherlands. The Port of Rotterdam is one of the most important tourist attractions in the Netherlands. Rotterdam is the second largest port in the world.

Tourism in Utrecht

Utrecht is one of the most beautiful tourist cities in the Netherlands. It is characterized by its historical and religious character, which is evident in its old town, which includes ancient historical buildings dating back to the Middle Ages, such as the Dom Cathedral.

Rotterdam port

One of the largest ports in the world, it dates back to the fourteenth century AD and occupies a very large area. It is also considered one of the most famous tourist places in Rotterdam. The tour takes about an hour or more in the port, where you will have a view of the wonderful Rotterdam skyline, as well as the giant shipyards and containers.

Euromast Rotterdam Tower

One of the most famous tourist attractions in Rotterdam. It is considered the tallest tower in the Netherlands, with a length of 185 meters. It is open to the public so that they can see the skyline of Rotterdam from its top. The Euromast tower overlooks the city center, the port of Rotterdam and the surrounding areas

Rotterdam Zoo

One of the oldest and most famous zoos in the Netherlands. It is a tourist attraction in Rotterdam. It is located north of Rotterdam Station and is frequented by many visitors, whether local residents or tourists. Rotterdam Zoo is an ideal choice for outings with family and children.

Rotterdam market

Rotterdam Market or Market Hall is a huge building in the form of an arch, the building includes apartments and offices in addition to a large market and many shops and cafes, which number up to one hundred.

Erasmus Berg Bridge

A bridge linking the northern part to the southern part of the city of Rotterdam. It is considered one of the most important symbols of the city. It was designed by Ben van Berkel. Its construction was completed in 1996. This bridge was called the Swan because of the shape of its design.

kenderdijk mills

A suburb consisting of 19 old mills dating back to 1740, which is one of the most important tourist attractions in Rotterdam and has been classified by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site. Visitors are introduced to the history and origins of these mills, as they are certainly one of the most important symbols of the city of Rotterdam
